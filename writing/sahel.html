Political Risks Facing Chinese Mining Investments in the Sahel

Research
Paper

Executive Summary

Chinese mining investments in the Sahel are expanding amid growing political instability, military coups, resource nationalism, and insurgent violence. This assessment examines the risks affecting Chinese companies operating in Mali, Niger, Burkina Faso, and neighbouring states.

Key Risks

  • Political instability and changing mining agreements
  • Resource nationalism and local opposition
  • Insurgent violence affecting operations
  • Weak institutional environments

Conclusion

The Sahel presents significant opportunities for resource investment, but political and security dynamics increasingly shape the operating environment for foreign companies.
